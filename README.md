A simple, effective, and versatile design document for games, utilities, and other digital products.


Project Title
=============

* * *

### **_Overview:_**

Give a high-level description of your project. This should be neutral-toned and professional. The purpose of this description is to give the reader an introductory idea of what the project is. This should be written similarly to the introduction of a research paper. In the overview, introduce the ideas that will be covered in later sections, but do not describe these ideas in detail yet.

* * *

Internal Details
----------------

### **_Publisher:_**

 List the company or person your product is being published by.

### **_Team:_**

List all the member of the team and their role. Order these any way you like. Some examples would be alphabetical by first/last name, grouped by role, etc.

*   Jane Doe - Lead Developer
    
*   John Smith - Art Director
    
*   Alex Hamilton - Storyboarder
    

  

### **_Goals:_**

What do you as an individual/team want to achieve with this project? Do you want to improve certain skills; do you want to create this product for a specific reason; do you want to something to fulfil a specific market? Whatever your reason is for creating this product is, describe that here.

  

### **_Tools:_**

List the tools you will use to create this product. This includes programming languages, frameworks, engines, asset creation tools, and external sources. Provide the purpose alongside the tool.

*   Unity - Game Engine
    
*   C# - Programming Language
    
*   Affinity - Graphics
    
*   [OpenGameArt.net](http://OpenGameArt.net) - Graphical Assets - External Source
    
*   Audacity - Audio
    
*   Google Ads API - Advertisements
    
*   Ellipsus - Planning & Design
    

  

### **_Platform:_**

 What platform will this product be released on? PC, mobile, web, console?

* * *

External Details
----------------

### **_Audience:_**

What demographic is this product for? Why? Consider age, gender, socioeconomic background, occupation, etc. Explain why this is your target audience and how your product appeals to them specifically.

  

### **_Theme & Genre/Purpose:_**

 What is the theme of your product? Consider your inspirations and products of similar design. What is the common factor between them?

If your product is a game, what is the main genre of the game? Does it fall into a subgenre?

If your product is a software, what is the purpose of your software? What is it used for?

  

### **_Visual Style:_**

 Describe the appearance of your product. Do the graphics follow a specific theme? What are your inspirations for visuals? Why did you choose this visual style?

  

### **_Audio Design:_**

Does your product have audio? Why or why not? What is the style of your audio? Modern; Chiptune; Clean; Distorted?

  

### **_Features & Mechanics:_**

 Describe the features and mechanics in your product. What actions can the user perform? What interactions are there between the user and the product? Are there interactions between elements of the product that don't involve user input? List everything you can think of, even if it seems implied. Make the first 3-5 items in the list the most important things; your key features. Bold or highlight them to indicate this.

  

### **_Standout Element:_**

 What makes your product different that other similar products? Why would a user choose this product over another?

Game Details
------------

The following section only applies to games. If you are not developing a game, feel free to delete this section. Or, if you see something in this section that would apply to your product, feel free to save it and fill it out anyway. After all, this is your product and your design document.

### **_Core Gameplay Loop:_**

List the actions the player will take and repeat throughout the course of a play session. Provided is an example game loop for a simple 2D platformer.

*   Move through the level
    
*   Fight enemies
    
*   Collect coins
    
*   Collect powerups
    
*   Fight boss
    
*   Complete level
    
*   Proceed to next level
    
*   Repeat
    

  

### **_Win & Lose Conditions:_**

How does the player win the game? This could be earning a certain number of points, reaching the end of a level, or surviving a wave of enemies.

How does the player lose the game? This could be running out of time or running out of lives.

It is also important to consider that not all games have win or lose conditions. Some games just exist to be experienced without either, and that is perfectly fine.

  

**_Story:_**

What is the basic idea for the story of the game? How does the player impact it? Summarize the story here. Create a secondary document to write your entire story. Link that document here as well.

  

### **_World:_**

 Describe the world your game takes place in. Include the main setting as well as other areas included in the game. Also include areas that are part of the game world but not physically in the game. The best way to make your world feel immersive is to have your world exist outside of what the player sees. This makes the player feel as though they are part of something larger than what they see.

  

### **_Key Experiences:_**

 Key experiences are things you want the player to experience or feel when playing your game. You should aim to have around 3 of these. Some games aim to leave you with a sense of accomplishment. Others just want to exist as a stimulating time-killer. And then there are games that aim to change your way of thinking on a funamental level. Then there are also games that exist purely to incite rage and frustration. All of these are valid key experiences. But the most important thing is that you try to have at least one of your key experiences be something authentic; something that sets you apart from other games in your genre.

  

### **_Primary Objective:_**

 What is the end goal for the player? Some examples include rescuing a princess, defeating a great evil, or safely escaping an alien planet.

  

### **_Supporting Objectives:_**

 Aside from the main objective, are there any smaller tasks that the player is trying to complete? Some examples could be collecting special items, rescuing NPCs from monsters, or building a town for NPCs and ensuring they are happy. Consider the impact of these supporting objectives on the game world. Does completing the supporting objectives give a reward or potentially lead to a special event or a new ending?

  

### **_Enablers & Blockers:_**

 What factors aid or hinder the player's objectives? Consider active and passive factors for both.

*   Enablers
    
    *   Active
        
        *   Power-ups
            
        *   Special Abilities
            
        *   Skill Upgrades
            
    *   Passive
        
        *   Checkpoints
            
        *   Rest Areas
            
*   Blockers
    
    *   Active
        
        *   Enemies
            
        *   Boss Fights
            
        *   Difficult Platforming
            
    *   Passive
        
        *   Limited Lives
            
        *   Time Limits
            
        *   Locked Areas

### **_Pitch:_**

 Write a pitch for your product. This should be between 5 and 10 minutes long. This should be get someone excited about your product. Leave out technical details. This should be a high-level description of your product that focuses on what makes it great. This should be able to appeal to potential publishers, clients, and users. Create a slideshow to go along with it and link it here. Visual aids are very important and are often undervalued by speakers despite making a huge difference.

  

### **_Final Notes:_**

 Include anything here that wasn't covered above. Feel free to add missing sections if needed. Also, remember that a design document is meant to be a living document. This document is not finalized until the project is fully complete. And even then, it is not finalized if you plan to continue support via updates.
